“The only way to do great work is to love what you do. If you haven’t found it yet, keep looking. Don’t settle.” –Steve Jobs

I have found what I love to do – build things. A year ago, I started a journey to change careers from owning my own medical transcription company to learning to code. I found my passion when I took my first classes at Metropolitan Community College in programming. After finding that spark I signed up at AIM Code School to learn the Foundations of Web Development followed by full stack .NET. At AIM I really jumped into building projects and working with colleagues to solve complicated problems. After completing my degree at Metropolitan Community College I decided to continue to upgrade my knowledge by pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Web Development at Bellevue University.

My experience owning my own small business for the past 13 years has provided me opportunities many haven’t had. I have worked with a wide variety of clients with different specialties and levels of professional achievement to gather requirements and produce diverse deliverables. My adaptability and attention to detail (99.9% transcription accuracy rate) are essential skills that allow me to make updates to my code on the fly and adapt to new requirements while still maintaining clean, working code.

My CliftonStrengths top five includes Learner, Ideation, and Achiever, and my love of learning and web development don’t stop with Bellevue University or AIM Code School. I have attended meet-ups, conferences, and utilize several self-paced learning resources on the internet. I am driven to learn about new languages and technologies, having recently earned my Amazon AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner certification with a goal of attaining Certified Developer status. I am also familiar with both jQuery and Bootstrap. I am building on that JavaScript knowledge by learning Vue and Angular.
